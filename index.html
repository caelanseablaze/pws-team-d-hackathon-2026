Welcome to Adventure AID!

You wake up in a strange-looking, yet familiar landscape, wearing outlandish clothes you didn't own before. Checking your pocket, you find a lightly crumpled map. As you stare at it in confusion, a purple dotted line begins to make its way across...

...Perhaps if you go on this adventure, you'll find your place in this world.

This is a prototype submitted by PAREF Woodrose Senior Team D for the 2026 Breaking Barriers, Building Bridges Hackathon! Because of this, refreshing the page will not save changes you make.

Adventure AID is a game where you explore and rate the accessibility of real-world public spaces. You won't be alone though–wherever you go, you might find fantastical creatures to accompany you. Plus, playing and contributing ratings also helps everyone, particularly Persons With Disability (PWDs) to navigate, access safe, inclusive spaces, and overall have a little more whimsy in life.

To get you started, consider checking out your Map. There are two sample locations for you to test out the rating feature. You can also check out the Creatures you've collected, or read up on some Information. The choice is yours!

In the future, you may also opt to input some of your health information in your Account. This feature isn't fully implemented yet, but it will be used with Artificial Intelligence (AI) to provide recommendations for spaces that suit your specific needs. This will be secure, but you would be able to opt out of this at any time. For now, the prototype has no "real AI" and any information you provide is stored solely in this page.

Rating A Location

To rate a nearby location, click on its corresponding pin on the map. Then, tap the button that reads "Rate This Location." You will then be prompted to submit a rating for this location by answering "Yes" or "No" to a number of accessibility criteria. Try to assess whether the space is usable for people with mobility problems, vision problems, or other disabilities.

Responsible Rating

In order to ensure rating accuracy and usability for other users, we highly encourage users to keep these guidelines in mind while rating locations. In the future, we will add the ability to upload photos of a location in order to further verify their ratings. Note that this is based on Philippine law, more specifically Batas Pambansa (BP) 344 and Republic Act (RA) 7277, the Magna Carta for Disabled Persons. However, Adventure AID is not affiliated with the government. If you would like more details, researching these laws is a good place to start.

Accessibility Standards Guide

  • Ramps
    • Minimum clear width: 1.20 m (1200 mm)
    • Maximum gradient:
      • Preferred: 1:20
      • Maximum: 1:12 (special cases depending on vertical length)
    • Landings required every 1.50 m, 1.80 m, or 2.0 m vertical rise
    • Minimum landing length: 1.50 m
    • Required whenever there is a change in level unless served by:
      • Dropped sidewalk
      • Curb ramp
      • Elevator
      • Mechanical device
    • Complementary ramps, lifts, or escalators required when staircases exist along accessible routes
  • Walkways
    • Minimum width: 1.20 m
    • Maximum gradient: 1:20
    • Must be smooth, unobstructed, and barrier-free
  • Doors & Entrances
    • Minimum clear door width:
      • 900 mm (standard)
      • 1800 mm (double doors)
    • Handles positioned between 900 mm – 1200 mm above floor
    • Accessible entrances:
      • All public entrances must be accessible (except restricted/service)
      • Accessible route must be direct (no detours)
      • Ramps/lifts required if entrance is not level
      • Vestibules must allow wheelchair maneuvering space
      • Weather protection required (rain & heat)
    • Door Requirements:
      • Adequate clear floor space before and after door
      • Low opening force (automatic preferred)
      • Hardware usable without tight grasping or twisting
      • Slow-closing swing doors for safety
      • Swing-out doors for toilets, storage, closets
      • Kick plates on major circulation routes
      • No sharp edges
      • 30% visual contrast for doors, frames, hardware
    • Vision & Safety:
      • Vision panels required on accessible-route doors
      • Panels visible to wheelchair users
      • Glass doors must have visible high-contrast markings
    • Automatic Doors:
      • Must allow safe passing time
      • Must work manually if power fails
      • Control buttons reachable and easy to use
    • Thresholds:
      • Maximum height: 12 mm
      • Beveled if over 6 mm
  • Toilets & Bathrooms
    • Minimum size: 1.70 m × 1.80 m
    • Grab bars required
    • At least one toilet seat at 0.45 m height
  • Parking
    • Accessible parking slots: 3.70 m wide
    • Located near entrances
    • Clear signage required
  • Handrails
    • Installed at 0.70 m and 0.90 m above floor
    • 50 mm clearance from wall
  • Signage
    • International Symbol of Access required for accessibility features
    • Directional and information signage required
    • Minimum 30% color contrast
    • Letter size proportional to viewing distance
    • Mounting height: max 1500 mm (center of sign)
    • Minimum sign size: 200 mm × 200 mm
    • Door signs for accessible toilets/showers: 200 mm × 200 mm
    • Symbols should face right (use arrows if left)
    • Must include graphics, text, embossed tactile characters (min 1 mm height), and Braille
    • Tactile maps required for visually impaired users
    • Signs must not:
      • Protrude more than 100 mm from walls
      • Be mounted between 400 mm – 2000 mm height in hazardous ways
      • Intrude into walkways below 2200 mm clearance
  • Streets & Pedestrian Crossings
    • Must be barrier-free
    • Cut-out curbs and ramps required
    • Audio-visual pedestrian crossing aids required
    • Accessibility applies to newly constructed areas
    • When streets are repaired or renovated, accessibility must be added if crossings are affected
    • Overpasses/underpasses may be exempt in some cases
  • Public Transportation
    • No franchise/license unless:
      • Designated seating for PWDs provided
      • Audio-visual aids installed
    • Applies to:
      • Buses
      • Passenger boats & ships
      • Domestic airplanes
      • Trains (including light rail)
    • Existing vehicles being repaired or renovated must comply
  • Public Telephones
    • At least 1 in every 4 must be accessible
    • If only one exists, it must be accessible
    • Must include visual aids
  • Public Transport Terminals
    • Must follow accessibility standards for public buildings
    • Applies to bus, train, ship, and airport terminals

How to Play

Click on a nearby map pin to view its accessibility ratings. The percentages reflect the number of users who rated "yes" for a given feature.

Press "Rate This Location" to submit your own rating.

Click "yes" or "no" to indicate your rating. You may leave some parts blank if you are unsure.

Please answer to the best of your ability, and try to consider the guidelines in this article.

Submit a complete rating to earn a new creature! If you left any fields blank, you will not earn a new creature until you go back and update the rating.
